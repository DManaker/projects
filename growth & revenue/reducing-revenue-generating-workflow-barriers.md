# Reducing Revenue-Generating Workflow Barriers

## ✖️ Problem
Operators were spending excessive time navigating multi‑step workflows to confirm caregivers for shifts, especially for repeat caregivers they already trusted. Each additional review step introduced friction in a revenue‑critical path, increasing the likelihood that operators abandoned the process or delayed confirmations.

This created compounding issues:
* Lower conversion of posted → filled shifts
* Increased operator frustration and platform fatigue
* Slower time‑to‑value despite a strong supply of qualified clinicians

The problem wasn’t clinician quality—it was workflow friction in high‑frequency actions.

## 💡 Solution
I owned the project end‑to‑end, from discovery through delivery, to redesign this revenue‑generating workflow with a focus on speed, trust, and automation without sacrificing compliance.

Key improvements included:
* Identifying and removing unnecessary friction points in shift confirmation flows
* Streamlining B2B ↔ B2C interactions to reduce repetitive decision‑making
* Introducing a trusted clinician model that allowed operators to automatically accept repeat clinicians when predefined criteria were met
* Preserving critical compliance visibility while eliminating over‑protective steps that actively worked against speed and efficiency

The result was a faster, more intuitive workflow that respected operator judgment while maintaining platform safeguards.

## 💥 Impacts & Results
* 17% of all posted shifts adopted the trusted caregiver flow post‑launch
* Significant increase in conversion of outstanding shifts to filled shifts
* Reduced operator‑initiated cancellations by removing confirmation delays
* Created a more consistent experience for caregivers, contributing to lower B2C attrition
