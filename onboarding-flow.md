# Standardizing Onboarding Workflows

## ✖️ Problem
As KARE scaled, community onboarding was handled inconsistently across customers and internal teams. With no unified workflows or shared standards in place:
* Onboarding experiences varied widely depending on who owned the process
* Adoption and retention outcomes were unpredictable
* New hires relied on tribal knowledge, increasing ramp time and operational risk

The risk became more apparent - as volume increased, inconsistent onboarding would directly impact customer confidence, product adoption, and the company’s ability to scale efficiently.

## 💡 Solution
I owned the design and rollout of standardized onboarding workflows to reduce variance, improve consistency, and enable scale. Rather than treating onboarding as a one‑off process, I approached it as a repeatable system by:
* Creating template‑driven onboarding workflows and diagrams for company‑wide reference
* Defining key onboarding events and implementing automated, event‑triggered communications to ensure consistent touchpoints
* Developing directional guides and enablement materials to support training, handoffs, and new‑hire ramp

The goal was not just documentation, but a shared operating model that allowed teams to deliver a consistent experience while still iterating based on feedback and performance data.

## 💥 Results and Impact
* Established a unified onboarding framework that reduced experience variability across customer segments
* Enabled teams to identify weak points in onboarding and iterate using feedback and adoption signals
* Improved onboarding scalability by reducing dependency on individual team members or tribal knowledge
* Supported organizational growth by allowing new onboarding team members to ramp quickly and deliver consistent outcomes
