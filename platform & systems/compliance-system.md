# Sharing Liability: Compliance as a Growth Lever

## ✖️ Problem

In senior care staffing, most gig nursing platforms push compliance responsibility like background checks, identity verification, certifications, and state‑mandated training, entirely onto operators.

While this reduces platform liability and risk for the gig nursing company, it creates significant friction for operators and limits adoption, particularly for larger communities operating across multiple states with enterprise level systems.

For KARE, this created a clear opportunity and constraint:
* Sales and GTM were blocked in compliance‑heavy states involving enterprise deals
* Operators viewed compliance as operational risk rather than platform value
* Existing onboarding systems were not flexible enough to support full state‑specific labor laws

To differentiate in a competitive market, KARE needed to share compliance responsibility without introducing legal or operational risk.

## 💡 Solution

I led continuous product discovery and delivery iterating on a compliance management system that transformed regulatory complexity into a GTM advantage.

My role focused on translating legal and operational requirements into scalable product capabilities by:
* Partnering with compliance leadership, legal labor experts, and operators to define where KARE could safely assume shared responsibility
* Running discovery workshops, writing PRDs, and validating prototypes with real operator workflows
* Designing an onboarding framework that supported state‑specific configuration rather than one‑size‑fits‑all rules that didn't impede internal operations

### Key Capabilities Delivered

* Configurable caregiver onboarding requirements by state, including:
  * Document collection and validation, including KYE integrations
  * Implement state‑mandated screenings into our user interface
  * Unique certification and testing requirement proof documentation
* A system design for continous iteration, accomodating evolving labor laws without requiring code rebuilds.
* Support for 30+ states, each with distinct regulatory constraints, while maintaining accuracy and auditability.

Rather than adding friction, the system absorbed compliance complexity on behalf of operators, reducing their operational burden and increasing trust in the platform.

## 💥 Impacts & Results

* Enabled a differentiated GTM strategy centered on shared compliance responsibility, positioning KARE against major market competitors
* Unlocked new sales opportunities and supported enterprise expansion
* Added over 3000+ senior care communities, driven by improved onboarding confidence and reduced compliance risk
* Strengthened partnerships between product, sales, and marketing by providing a clear compliance narrative for customer acquisition
