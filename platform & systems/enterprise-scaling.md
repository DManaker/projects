# Scaling Enterprise Capabilities

## ✖️ Problem
KARE’s marketplace was originally designed for local, single‑site operators. As our GTM strategy shifted toward multi‑site and enterprise customers, the platform’s architecture introduced significant friction during acquisition and onboarding.
Enterprise users—such as C‑suite and VP‑level stakeholders—were limited to read‑only access with minimal operational control. This created immediate post‑sale friction, slowed onboarding, and undermined confidence in the platform.

Additionally, legacy business rules and UI constraints made enterprise setup unnecessarily difficult, including:
* Rigid 1‑user‑to‑1‑operator logic
* Duplicate account creation and workarounds during onboarding
* Restrictive permission models that prevented centralized management
* Federation and identity duplication issues across integrated platforms

These limitations directly impacted customer experience, sales velocity, and the company’s ability to scale enterprise adoption.

## 💡 Solution
I led multiple phases of work to transform the marketplace from a local‑first system into an enterprise‑ready platform with scalable control, permissions, and onboarding.

Key initiatives included:
* Designing enterprise‑level control capabilities that allowed centralized oversight across multiple child communities
* Re‑architecting permission models to support individualized, role‑based access rather than static, company‑defined rules
* Removing onboarding barriers and restrictive business logic that prevented enterprise users from onboarding and managing local operators on their behalf
* Partnering with engineering to resolve federation and identity duplication issues, enabling seamless cross‑platform usage with our scheduling software

The focus was on eliminating structural blockers, not adding complexity. By doing so, enterprise customers could onboard quickly and operate with confidence.

## 💥 Impacts & Results
* Accelerated enterprise onboarding by removing setup workarounds and permission bottlenecks
* Increased confidence in enterprise‑focused GTM strategy by delivering operational parity with customer expectations
* Enabled revenue growth from multi‑site customers through scalable account management and control
* Improved internal sales and onboarding efficiency by aligning platform capabilities with enterprise deal expectations
