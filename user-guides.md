# Product Knowledge Hub & Customer Knowledge Base

## ✖️ Problem
When I joined KARE, product knowledge was fragmented across multiple platforms. Internally, teams struggled to find accurate, up‑to‑date feature information. Externally, customers lacked a centralized source for product guidance.

This created compounding issues:
* Increased support volume for basic product questions
* Slower internal response times due to inconsistent information
* Customer friction caused by unclear product usage and feature understanding

As product complexity grew, the absence of a scalable knowledge system became both a support and customer experience risk.

## 💡 Solution
I led the design and rollout of a two‑pronged knowledge management system:
1. An internal product hub serving as a single source of truth for teams
2. A customer‑facing knowledge base enabling self‑service support and education

The internal hub consolidated release notes, feature documentation, and technical specifications to improve internal alignment. The customer‑facing platform provided intuitive access to guides, walkthroughs, and troubleshooting resources designed to reduce dependency on support.

## 💥 Results and Impact
* Reduced support tickets related to common product questions through effective self‑service resources
* Improved response times and consistency for internal teams by establishing a single source of truth
* Enabled better feature adoption and smoother customer onboarding through clearer documentation
* Created a scalable foundation for customer education and future product launches
