# Establishing a Shared User Persona Database

## ✖️ Problem
As KARE scaled, teams lacked a shared understanding of who our customers were and how they should be approached. Different departments operated with conflicting assumptions about ideal customers, use cases, and value propositions.

This led to several compounding issues:
* Misaligned go‑to‑market messaging and inconsistent sales targeting
* Varying account management strategies that increased churn risk
* Friction during deployments caused by mismatched expectations and improper product positioning

As the prospective customer base expanded, the absence of standardized user segmentation became a growth constraint, slowing GTM execution and diluting product messaging.

## 💡 Solution
I owned the research, creation, and rollout of a centralized user persona database designed to act as a company‑wide source of truth for customer understanding.

Rather than treating personas as static documentation, I focused on creating a shared reference system that could inform decisions across product, sales, marketing, and deployments by:
* Conducting customer research and synthesizing patterns across customer types and use cases
* Defining clear persona attributes, needs, and success criteria tied to real customer behavior
* Publishing and embedding the persona library in a centralized SharePoint location to ensure accessibility and adoption

The goal was to align teams around who we were building for, selling to, and supporting—using a common language.

## 💥 Results and Impact
* Improved alignment across product, sales, and marketing on ideal customer profiles and messaging
* Increased GTM cohesion by enabling consistent prospecting and positioning strategies
* Reduced friction during deployments by setting clearer expectations around customer needs and product fit
* Supported higher‑quality sales conversations by better matching product capabilities to user expectations
