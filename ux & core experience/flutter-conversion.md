# Framework Upgrade for Mobile Application

## ✖️ Problem
As KARE’s B2C product matured, continued feature additions exposed structural limitations in the existing React Native framework. Performance degraded, UI consistency varied across screens, and third‑party library integration became increasingly fragile.

These technical constraints created compounding business risk:
* Increased app instability and downtime
* Slower development velocity and longer debugging cycles
* Degraded user experience leading to higher attrition
* Declining app store ratings that negatively impacted acquisition

It became clear that incremental fixes would not address the underlying platform instability.

## 💡 Solution
I led the initiative to modernize our mobile platform by migrating from React Native to Flutter, treating the effort as a product stabilization and retention investment, not a technical rewrite.

Partnering closely with engineering, I helped drive the decision and execution to:
* Improve performance and UI consistency across Android and iOS
* Reduce long‑term maintenance burden and third‑party dependency risk
* Establish a more stable foundation for future feature development

My ownership included:
* Defining scope, milestones, and delivery sequencing within our dev ops system
* Partnering with engineering to create user stories, acceptance criteria, and release plans
* Coordinating cross‑functional launch readiness, including support, marketing, and operations
* Designing rollback strategies and staged rollouts to mitigate launch risk
* Overseeing beta testing across platforms to ensure production‑ready quality
* Identifying and simplifying confusing user workflows during the migration

## 💥 Impacts & Results
* App store ratings improved to 4.5+, up from the mid‑3 range, strengthening acquisition and brand trust
* Reduced post‑onboarding drop‑off by 45%, signaling improved early user experience
* Increased average customer lifetime by ~1 additional month
* Decreased technical support inquiries by 30%+, reducing operational load
